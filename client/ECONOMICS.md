# Economics

I would model this tool as a lead-generation surface for high-intent buyers of discounted AI credits, not as a standalone SaaS product. The key question is simple: what is a converted lead worth to Credex? A rough but usable assumption is that a customer who buys discounted credits through Credex moves at least $20,000 of annual AI spend. If Credex captures, for example, a 12% gross margin or equivalent spread on that transaction volume, a converted customer is worth about $2,400 in gross profit contribution over a year. If higher-spend customers move $50,000 to $100,000 annually, that figure climbs quickly, but I would anchor on the lower number to stay conservative.

Now work backward. Suppose 100 completed audits generate 15 report saves, 6 Credex consultations, and 2 closed purchases. At $2,400 gross profit each, those 2 wins are worth $4,800. That means each completed audit is worth about $48 in expected gross profit contribution. Even if those assumptions are optimistic, the room is large enough that a low-cost inbound tool can work.

CAC by channel should stay close to zero for the first wave because the distribution plan is content, communities, and founder-led outreach. A realistic estimate:

- X launch thread and follow-up screenshots: effectively $0 cash CAC, but high founder time cost.
- Indie Hackers / Reddit / Hacker News comments: $0 cash CAC, medium time cost.
- Direct outreach in startup Slack groups and founder communities: also $0 cash CAC, but highest time cost.

If I assign a notional internal cost of $75/hour and estimate 20 hours to get the first 100 users, the blended acquisition spend is about $1,500 of time. If that yields 100 users, CAC per audit visitor is $15. If 25% complete an audit, CAC per completed audit is $60. That is slightly above the $48 expected value estimate above, which means the funnel would need to perform a bit better or the converted customer value would need to be a bit higher. That is healthy pressure: it keeps the model honest.

What conversion rate makes this profitable? Using the same $2,400 value per converted customer, and a $60 CAC per completed audit, you need one closed customer per 40 completed audits to break even. That is a 2.5% audit-to-purchase conversion rate. If the consultation booking rate is 8% and the consultation-to-purchase close rate is 30%, then audit -> purchase is 2.4%, which is very close. The product only needs to be slightly better than that to be attractive.

To reach $1M ARR in 18 months, assume Credex recognizes about $2,400 gross profit contribution per converted customer per year from this channel. That requires about 417 active annual customers attributable to the tool. Over 18 months, that could mean roughly 28 net new customers per month on average. If audit-to-purchase conversion settles at 2.5%, the tool would need around 1,120 completed audits per month. That is aggressive but not absurd if the product becomes a recognizable benchmark surface and Credex compounds distribution with content, partnerships, and public benchmark reports.
